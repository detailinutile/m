Mario Wimmer

Deputy director of the Collegium Helveticum · Swiss Institute of Advanced Study

About

As a historian, I am working on the conditions that facilitate intellectual work. At the Collegium Helveticum, I put these insights into practice by facilitating intellectual exchange among international fellows, the local academic community, and the wider public.

more

Since receiving a doctorate in modern history in 2010, I have worked in research and teaching at ETH Zurich, UC Berkeley, and the University of Basel. I have published on how modern societies engage with their past, the changing conditions of administrative and intellectual work, and the history of media. I also have experience as curator and continue working with artists at the Collegium.

In 2022, I joined the Collegium Helveticum as executive director, and am responsible for its academic and artistic program. Together with my highly motivated colleagues, we have succeeded in positioning the Collegium as one of the leading institutes for advanced study, working each year with a cohort of thirty to forty international fellows from all disciplines, ranging from the natural sciences and engineering, life sciences and medicine, and the social sciences, economics, and law to the humanities and the arts.

Over the past years, I helped start and secure funding for a scholars at risk program and serve on the board of the Virtural Ukrainian Institute of Advanced Study, based at the Wissenschaftskolleg zu Berlin. Currently, I co-chair a working group with Sophie Halart for NETIAS. In September 2025, the Collegium entered a partnership with the NOMIS Foundation, further strengthening its fellowship program.
